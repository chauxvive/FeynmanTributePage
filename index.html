Richard Feynman

A life well-lived

Feynman understood how to work hard AND play hard-
and sometimes it seemed like he barely knew the difference between the two!

Richard Feynman was an American theorectical physicist known for his work in quantum mechanics. He assisted in the building of the atom bomb, picked highly secured locks and played bongos in parades

A Timeline of Feynman's Life and Work:

  • 1918 Feynman is born in Queens, NYC
  • 1921 Feynman says his first word
  • 1933 Feynman teaches himself trigonometry, advanced algebra, infinite series, analytic geometry, and integral and differential calculus. Like you do
  • 1936 Feynman is turned down from Columbia because they've already fufilled their Jewish quota for the year. He goes to MIT instead
  • 1936-1939 Feynman switches from majoring in mathematics to electrical engineering to physics
  • 1939 Feynman aces the Princeton entrance exam in physics but does poorly on history and English. Again, he was almost not let in because he was Jewish (although a professed atheist)
  • 1939 Feynman has a seminar with Einstein. The rules of his scholarship dictate he may not be married to his sweetheart, who is dying of tuberculosis
  • 1941 Feynman is recruited to the Manhattan Project
  • 1942 Having received his doctorate, Feynman marries Arlene Greenbaum in the city office with no friends or family present. He brings her to the hospital, where her prognosis is poor and he may only visit on weekends
  • 1943 A bored Feynman takes up lock-picking as a hobby, breaking into top secret areas for fun
  • 1944 Fellow physicist Klaus Fuchs speculates Feynman may be a spook which ensures the FBI get a full file on Feynman. It is later revealed to be Fuchs that was the spook
  • 1945 Feynman's wife Arlene dies. The first atom bomb is tested
  • 1949 Feynman returns from Rio with a Brazilian girl and a new passion for bongo drums. He is still of high interest to the FBI
  • 1952 Feynman marries Mary Bell. They divorce in 1958
  • 1953 Feynman starts teaching at Caltech
  • 1960 Feynman creates a now famous curriculum overhaul which survives as The Feynman Lectures on Physics. His knack for breaking complex ideas into simple pieces and comparisons makes his work still some of the most accessible high level physics work today
  • 1960s Feynman begins granting interviews that become Surely You're Joking, Mr Feynman!
  • 1988 Feynman dies after a ten year battle with cancer. He said he "had told so many stories to so many people, even when he died he wouldn't be really gone." His legacy proves this is still true today

His last words were "I'd hate to die twice. It's so boring."

“Study hard what interests you the most in the most undisciplined, irreverent and original manner possible.”
